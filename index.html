This is the part where I talk about me. Tell you that I am a full stack software engineer, even though I have not been hired for that role yet. That I enjoy solving how to do things, and always improving in what I do, or I get bored. That's probably why I like playing the board game Go so much.

Probably good traits to have when you're legally blind and hard of hearing. As you can guess that does give you problems, and you're better off if you try to do as much as you can to limit its effects. Cell phone cameras were never made for the blind people, but they are great for us. My VR headset was made for games, I saw it as an assistive technology. Can I really have such a big screen I can move around? Yes, I can. Can I really have a my books with text as large has I need them with an ipad's kindle app? Yes, the best assistive technology for being legally blind were never intended to be. I have stubbornly tried to go up to the line of what I can do.
